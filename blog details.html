Prime Minister Dahal wins trust vote


Dahal receives 157 votes in the 275-member parliament. A total of 110 lawmakers voted against and one abstained.


Prime Minister Pushpa Kamal Dahal pictured amid lawmakers after winning the vote of confidence on Wednesday.

Post Report
Published at : March 13,2024
Updated at : March 14, 2024 13:28
Location : Kathmandu

Prime Minister Pushpa Kamal Dahal on Wednesday secured the vote of confidence in the House of Representatives. Dahal received 157 votes in the 275-member parliament. A total of 110 lawmakers voted against while a member abstained. A total of 268 lawmakers were present in todayâ€™s meeting. The prime minister required at least 138 votes to win the trust motion.

The prime minister sought a vote of confidence as the Nepali Congress withdrew its support to the government after he broke the alliance with Congress and joined hands with the CPN-UML and other parties on March 4. This is the third time he secured a vote of trust in the parliament after he became prime minister in December 2022 with the support of the CPN-UML, among other parties. Dahal had gone for the floor test for the first time on January 10, 2023. He had received 268 votes then. The Prime Minister had to take the floor test for the second time only three months later after he again broke ties with the CPN-UML and forged an alliance with Congress in March. He had received 172 votes at the time.

"Of 268 lawmakers who took part in the voting, 157 voted for the vote of confidence, which is a majority of the total existing lawmakers in the house," said Dev Raj Ghimire, speaker of the lower house. "Prime Minister Pushpa Kamal Dahal has won the vote of confidence." The support of 138 lawmakers is enough to win the vote of trust in the 275-member chamber. The vote of confidence became mandatory after Dahal broke alliance with the Nepali Congress, the largest party in the lower house, and reintroduced the Communist Party of Nepal (Unified Marxist-Leninist) into the new coalition on March 4 and formed a 22-member new cabinet days later. This was the third time for Dahal, chairman of CPN (Maoist Center), to win a vote of trust since he became the head of a coalition government comprising the CPN-UML in late December 2022 following the general election in November that year.
